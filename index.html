Table 1: Are Women Participating More or Less than Males?

The line chart above shows that both genders’ participations in Olympics are instantly growing from 1924 to 2014. It is clearly evident that the participation of women players in Olympics has significantly improved over the years.

For women, the number of participation tends to increase smoothly and gradually at the start. Then there is a change point in 1964, where women’s number of participation has an obvious rise. After 1994, the number of women who participated in the Olympics increases sharply.

For men, the upward trend tends to be more unsteady compared to women. There are several obvious drops at the first 40 years in 1928, 1936 and 1960. After 1960, the number of men that participated in the Olympics has shown a gradual ascending tendency.

Overall, the total participations in Olympics of men are much greater than that of women from 1924 to 2014. There is an obvious rapid rise in women’s participation after 1988, since the difference between men’s and women’s participation is gradually decreasing after 1988, which means that there is a rise of the social status and standings of modern women.
